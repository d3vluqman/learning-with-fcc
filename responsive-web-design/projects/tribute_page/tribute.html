Marie Curie: A Pioneer of Science

Marie Curie in her laboratory

Marie Curie was a physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity.
She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences: Physics and Chemistry.

Her work revolutionized the understanding of radiation and its applications in medicine and energy.
Curie's legacy continues to inspire generations of scientists worldwide.
